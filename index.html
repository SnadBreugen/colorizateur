What it does

The Color Guy reads an Audiotool project, groups all devices into four families (Synths, Drums, Audio Devices, VST), and writes coordinated colors to every cable, region and mixer strip in the project.

Workflow

  1. Log in with your Audiotool account.
  2. Paste a project URL and press Read.
  3. Pick colors per group — either by clicking the slots, or by using a preset, randomize, or your fav 3.
  4. Press Apply to write the colors to the project.

The three slots per group

  • Slot 1 — Main | Noteregion | Channels: direct audio cables out of the device, audio/note/pattern regions, mixer channels.
  • Slot 2 — FX Chain | Automation | Aux: cables running through FX, automation regions, aux strips.
  • Slot 3 — Midi FX | Groups: note cables and group strips.

Buttons

  • Apply — writes the current slot colors to the project.
  • Undo — reverts the last apply.
  • Randomize — picks twelve unique colors from the palette and assigns them across the project. The same twelve colors end up in the project and in the slots.
  • ★ Your Fav 3 — opens an editor to pick three colors. They get spread randomly across all cables, regions and strips.
  • Presets — five hand-picked color schemes that fill all slots at once.
  • Reset — clears all slot selections (does not change the project).

Notes

  • Cables routed into FX sends, master sends or aux sends keep their default gray color.
  • Empty groups (no devices in the project) are disabled and skipped.
  • Your fav 3 selections are stored locally in the browser.
